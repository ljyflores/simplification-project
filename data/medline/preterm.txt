Preterm labor is labor that starts before 37 completed weeks of pregnancy. It can lead to premature birth. Premature babies may face serious health risks. Symptoms of preterm labor include:Contractions every 10 minutes or more oftenLeaking fluid or bleeding from the vaginaFeeling of pressure in the pelvisLow, dull backacheCramps that feel like menstrual crampsAbdominal cramps with or without diarrhea If you think you might be having preterm labor, contact your health care provider. NIH: National Institute of Child Health and Human Development
